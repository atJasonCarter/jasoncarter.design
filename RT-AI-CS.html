TLDR:

I lead a small team to quickly build an AI wrapper for our customers to be able to manage their bookings without having to talk to Customer Services, find out about their destination and more.

Setup

From the begining

Taking ownership of the design meant be the second point of contact for all our clients, and the primary contact for our end-customer. Starting early on I interviewed our end-customers to see what they wanted as rewards, and how they would ideally like to interface with both obtaining the rewards and tracking/redeeming them. The team took those findings to our Pilot-Publishers (clients) to work on solutions that they wanted on their site. Engineering and Product Management also set up an automated A/B testing for copy and various designs to start tracking what was working best.

Example of Experiment Tracking
High-level Takeaways

1. Customers often had a preference in how they would like to earn rewards

1A. Reducing number of ads was most-requested, but customers couldn't acurately tell if they were seeing fewer ads.

2. Customers often had a unique preference in how they would like to earn rewards

3. Clients wanted to focus on getting customer registrations / Sign Ups

Round 1 Designs

Starting with introducing user registrations as our first product, we went through many itterations. Each itteration we were testing how to optomize the number of Sign-ins to "People who saw the offer"

Some design considerations
  • Incorporate Co-branding (publisher + bonbon should be present)
  • Get through registration as easily as possible
  • Any copy/ imagery/ logos/ promotions must be able to be automated or progromatically updated
Scroll to see variant progressions
Testing and Unexpected effects

Some inital key takeaways:

  • Imagery was a must, outperformed non-imagery modals by almost 2x.
  • Telling a story such as: "earn a date night", "win a year of hulu", outperformed simply explaining a benefit "win a $100 gift card".
  • Simplify, simplify, simplify. The fewer words, the better.
  • We outperformed expected sign up rates by ~150% by making these continual optomizations.
  • Our publishers weren't as excited at sign ups as they were by an unexpected secondary effect, users that were shown the modal visited 7% more pages.

Publishers asked us if we could increase that 7% number even further. We changed our buisness plan to focus on engagement. We started building out features that would increase engagement rates: bookmarking, commenting, voting, etc.

Meet the Bonbar

I designed a flexible system to fit each publishers wants. Some customers wanted bookmarking, some wanted a shopping cart or an account manager. I designed the Bonbar thoughtfully so that it was contextual based on what activity the user was doing with some simple logic gates our Engineering team set up.

By integrating more access points, we increased registrations by 280% (peaking at 1500% lift on launch). We also reduced customer annoyance by covering a smaller portion of the screen. Engagement shot through the roof to users visting 22% more pages, and we saw cross-network effects starting to happen (Users were more likely to visit another site that had Bonbar installed).
